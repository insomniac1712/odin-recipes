Napolitan Pizza

The authentic recipe of the famous Napolitan Pizza!

Authentic Neapolitan pizza originates from the birthplace of pizza, Naples. It involves simple ingredients and traditional methods that have been largely forgotten for quite a while.

Luckily it is bouncing back, with many people finding this beautiful style of pizza for the first time.

What makes Napolitan Pizza different?

The key characteristics of a perfect Neapolitan pizza are a really thin base with a crispy but light and airy crust.

The classic Neapolitan pizza is the Margherita. This is typically topped with tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, fresh basil, and extra virgin olive oil.

Parmesan cheese is also a popular addition as well as many other ingredients.

Ingredients

For Dough

  • 640 grams of flour
  • 360 grams of water
  • .14 grams of salt
  • 0.3 grams of yeast

For toppings

  • 300 grams of canned plum tomatoes (preferably San Marzano)
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato puree (optional)
  • Sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 fresh mozzarella balls (125 grams each)
  • 30 grams of Parmesan cheese
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Fresh basil leaves

Instructions

Prepare the Dough

  • In a large bowl, mix the flour, water, salt, and yeast until a dough forms.
  • Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rise at room temperature for about 8-24 hours.

Prepare the Sauce

  • Crush the canned tomatoes by hand or with a blender until smooth.
  • Add a pinch of sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  • Optionally, mix in the tomato puree for a richer flavor.

Shape the Dough

  • Preheat your oven to its highest setting (usually around 250°C or 482°F). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat up.
  • Divide the dough into 4 equal parts.
  • On a floured surface, stretch each dough ball into a thin, round shape, about 10-12 inches in diameter.

Assemble the Pizza

  • Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  • Tear the mozzarella into small pieces and distribute evenly over the sauce.
  • Grate the Parmesan cheese over the top.
  • Drizzle with a little extra virgin olive oil.

Bake the Pizza

  • Transfer the pizza to the preheated oven (or pizza stone) and bake for about 7-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  • Remove from the oven and top with fresh basil leaves.

Serve!

  • Drizzle a bit more olive oil over the top if desired.
  • Slice and enjoy your authentic Neapolitan pizza!

This recipe should give you a delicious, traditional Neapolitan pizza with a thin, crispy crust and fresh, flavorful toppings
